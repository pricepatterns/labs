Build A Web App For Publishing Training Docs

Distributing instructional content online, in a way that flows like it might with a live trainer can be a challenge for instructors. The COVID-19 pandemic has forced instructors, across all industries to migrate away from in-person training and into online learing environments. Video conference tools like Hangouts, Zoom and even YouTube provide audio and visual support, but what about the accompanying traning materials? Instructors need a digital framework to distribute written training materials. That's where Google Codelabs can help, as it provides a structured framework for delivering interactive and visually appealing training documents online. Codelabs helps instructors on any topic easily write lesson content in their Google Docs environment and then import that content into an application that students can use in any browser, and on any device. Below are just a few of the features of CodeLabs and why we used it's opensource code as the basis for our ed-teach app FireLabs.

Powerful, Familiar & Opensource

Although there are many ways to develop and distritube instructional content online, we wanted a fully inclusive soluton that would make it as easy as possible for subject matter expert insturctors to contribute content regardless of their ability to pay for web related services and without writing any code. G Suite, which includes Gmail, Google Docs and other apps seemed like a great place to start. It seemed eveyone we worked with had access and some level of familiarity with Gsuite, and the data supports our assumption as in 2020, Google reported more than 2 billion monthly active G Suite users. If you're familiar with the powerful and largely free to use tools available to you in your Google Cloud, then you have a great headstart. The content for each lesson or tutorial can be written just like any other document in your google docs accout making it a familiar place for instructors to write their content. There are some very simple formatting steps you'll need to take to prepare your google doc for export into your custom codelab website, however they are straightforward and largely repeatable steps which we will teach you in the next section.

In addition to being readily available and free for most people around the world to use, The underlying codebase that powers codelabs is completely opensource. This means that anyone can freely build a version of Codelabs that meets their unique needs and the needs of their student audiences.

Free & Inclusive Web Tools

As stated above, Google's G-suite and the underlying code that powers a codebase website are largely free for anyone, from anywhere in the world to use. This means that there are no licensing fees or out-of-pocket expenses that might prohibit someone from sharing what the know in a codelabs style website of their own. Generally, once you have some course content ready in Google Docs and your codelabs website ready to deploy, instructors will need a place to host the website and a domain name so students can access it online.

The cost to secure a domain name and host a website can vary dramatically from provider to provider, however, our mission here is to help anyone start a instructional website regarless of their ability to pay and of their ability to write code. So, for this tutorial we will introduce you to Freenom, a free domain name registrar and to Firebase, which offers a very generous free tier of backend hosting services. Combined, This tutorial we'll call "FireLabs" presents a solution that anyone wishing to author and share instructional content online can do it in a highly professional and secure way with no out of pocket costs.

Accessibility For Diverse Student Need Mobile, Desktop

Using responsive design techniques, your instructional website will function and look great on mobile and desktop screens, from a single code base and no need to build seperate website versions for different device types. If you're considering writing tutorials for students, maximumizing their ability to access the information is key as not all of your students will be accessing your lessons from a desk top or newest iPhone - being inclusive and eliminating barriers to entry for your students is key.

What you'll build

In this Firelabs tutorial, you're going to build you're very own version of Google's wildly successful CodeLabs website. Your version of the app will:

  • Help you build and share your own repositiry of learning content for any subject matter.
  • Use responsive design, so it works on desktop or mobile for maximum accessibility.
  • Be fast and secure.
  • Be customized to meet your branding needs.
  • Be easy for instructors write and publish content on regardless of their ability to code.
  • Be (to the best of our ability) 100% free for you to develop and host securly online.
